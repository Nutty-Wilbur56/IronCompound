Intrusion Prevention System: The Legionnaire

Unlike other VPNs, IronCompound utilizes an integrated Intrusion Prevention System; The Legionnaire, to monitor threats against the VPN server. What is an Intrusion Prevention System, you may ask...Intrusion Detection Systems are like advanced firewalls, but at the same time different. Intrusion Prevention Systems filter network traffic to root out malicious packets and or activity. Unlike regular firewalls that root out just the malicious packets, IPS's (at least mine is) are capable of barring potentially malicious clients from connecting to a server, router, or any other networking device. So far, the Legionnaire is designed to filter for SYN flood attacks and ICMP echo flood attacks. Apart from monitoring attacks, the IPS is also where most of the logging of security violations take place. Simple interactions with the server, such as initial connection, are logged with the server. One more use for that the IPS does is it tracks the list of active clients. The server, as previously mentioned, also tracks the amount of clients but for different purposes.

Artificial Intelligence

As described previously, The Legionnaire monitors network traffic for SYN flooding and ICMP echo flooding. In order for the Legionnaire to learn to filter for these attacks, the IPS is paired with a machine learning pipeline. As of right now the AI pipeline is fairly simple and rudimentary, given that the session data I am feeding the pipeline is pseudo data.

Rule Policy Engine: Caesar

Caesar, The Legionnaire's rule policy engine is a centralization of registering and adding the rules that The Legionnaire enforces. The engine also evaluates sessions every time a client interacts with the server.
